The Rubik's Cube was invented in 1975 by the Hungarian teacher, Ern≈ë Rubik. He wanted to make a mechanism of independently moving parts that wouldn't fall apart. Later, in 1979 the cube was introduced at the Nuremberg toy fair in Germany.

The cube was very popular and the company Ideal Toys signed a deal to manufacture and sell the cube worldwide. In 1980, the cube was sold internationally and the cube craze had begun! It was the most popular toy of the 1980s and has seen a significant revival in popularity in recent years.
